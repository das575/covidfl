(a) Florida Department of Health defines a negative test as "Total negative persons tested for all Florida and non-Florida residents." Calculated here, percent positive is total cases (residents and not) divided by (total cases plus total negatives). However, the calculation differs from the published number on the Florida Department of Health daily pdf, which calculates the percentage only for Florida residents. While non-residents have historically tested positive at a higher rate, the sheer numbers of non-residents are not large enough to make such a dramatic difference. This is an area for further study. For now, it's valid to compare the current day to the 7-day moving average or across the Florida regions, as defined here. However, percent positive should not be compared to other states or datasets.
